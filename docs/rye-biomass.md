
## Cereal Rye Termination Model

Predicts spring biomass accumulation of cereal rye cover crops using a logistic growth model. This model is not yet published and may change at any time. The model was trained on data from Wisconsin and may not reflect cover crop growth behavior outside of the upper Midwest ecoregion.

### Model details

The current model predicts biomass accumulation primarily through a logistic growth model using growing degree days since planting (base 0°C). Planting date and fall precipitation are also included (later planting dates and higher precipitation quantities will reduce spring biomass).

### Model threshold interpretation

- **Low biomass (<2000 lb/ac):** Cover crop may provide modest reduction in nitrate leaching, with limited reduction in soil erosion and phosphorus loss in runoff
- **Moderate biomass (2000-4500 lb/ac):** Cover crop may provide significant reduction in soil erosion, phosphorus loss in runoff, and nitrate leaching, with limited weed suppression
- **High biomass (>4500 lb/ac):** Cover crop may provide significant weed suppression, reduction in soil erosion, reduction in phosphorus loss in runoff, and mitigation of nitrate leaching
